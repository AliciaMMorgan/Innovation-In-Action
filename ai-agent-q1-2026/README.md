# Cross-Industry AI Agent — Q1 2026 Prototype

## Purpose

This work represents my Q1 2026 commitment to move from AI fluency study into hands-on agent construction. The goal was to translate lived cross-industry project, program, and portfolio leadership experience into an AI agent capable of guiding PMs through real-world AI adoption challenges.

---

## What Was Built

A prototype AI agent was developed in **Microsoft Azure AI Foundry (playground environment)**. The agent is designed to:

- Interpret real PM questions about AI adoption
- Identify situational context based on operating environment
- Provide structured guidance aligned to a staged AI-fluency journey
- Emphasize governance, risk awareness, and business value progression

The prototype validates that cross-industry PM pattern recognition can be encoded into agent behavior.

---

## Industry Context Informing the Build

At AgentCon Dallas 2025, **Microsoft leaders** outlined distinctions between copilots and full agent platforms, emphasizing that enterprise agent development requires environments supporting the full AI lifecycle, evaluation, safety testing, and scalable architecture.

These sessions clarified when to use Copilot Studio for conversational orchestration and when to use Azure AI Foundry for building, testing, and governing AI agents. This informed my decision to prototype in Foundry rather than a lightweight copilot builder.

Microsoft leaders also emphasized that there is no single agent approach that fits all organizational needs. Enterprise adoption spans no-code, low-code, and high-code solutions depending on complexity and risk. This reinforced that serious organizational agent development belongs in build-and-test platforms like Foundry.

In parallel, I completed International Institute for Learning (IIL) training aligned with PMI’s CPMAI framework. This introduced structured methods for categorizing AI initiatives into operational patterns and distinguishing project-level AI experiments from portfolio-level AI implementations. These frameworks now inform how Version 2 and Version 3 of this agent will be evaluated and evolved.

## What Happened and What It Proved

The prototype was validated using scenario-based acceptance tests focused on real PM constraints: business value proof, governance pressure, and influence without authority.

Across validation scenarios, the agent consistently demonstrated:

- Stage-aware guidance
- Context translation across operating environments
- PM-native language rather than AI hype
- Governance framed as an enabler rather than a blocker

This confirmed that cross-industry PM experience can be translated into consistent, situationally aware agent guidance.

---

## Patterns the Prototype Revealed

Validation surfaced a consistent underlying pattern:

**PMs are not struggling with AI tools. They are struggling with positioning AI inside organizational reality.**

Four repeatable patterns emerged:

1. **Stage confusion**  
   Teams often cannot distinguish experimentation from piloting or scale readiness. The agent clarified true adoption stage before recommending next steps.

2. **Business value translation gaps**  
   AI activity was easier to describe than AI value. The agent reframed work into executive-relevant outcomes such as cycle-time reduction, risk mitigation, cost avoidance, and throughput improvement.

3. **Governance timing misalignment**  
   Governance was either introduced too late or too early. The agent identified when governance becomes necessary and how to introduce it without stalling momentum.

4. **Influence without authority**  
   PMs lacked structured approaches to build executive trust in AI initiatives. The agent emphasized sequencing, stakeholder mapping, and credibility-building actions.

These patterns were consistent across environments, confirming transferability beyond a single industry context.
